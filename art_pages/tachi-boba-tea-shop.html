Taichi Bubble Tea Shop

Role Designer || Individual
Tools Arcylic Paint, Pencil

The Challenge

Customers at Taichi Bubble Tea were often confused about where to return their food trays after finishing their drinks, and delivery drivers were unsure where to pick up orders. This led to frequent questions at the counter and disrupted the flow of the space. The shop wanted signage that could solve this problem quickly and clearly, while still matching their brand. They specifically asked for a fun, cartoon-style design that followed the panda theme of the store, so the signs would feel welcoming and fit naturally into the environment rather than looking purely instructional.

The Approach

I began the project by meeting with the shop owner to discuss the purpose, placement, and size of the hanging counter signs. Together, we decided on two formats: 16 × 20 inches and 10 × 20 inches. Before painting, I sketched my ideas directly on the canvas and researched existing shop signage for inspiration. I shared these early sketches with the owner and adjusted the designs based on their feedback. While I was given artistic freedom, I followed the store’s panda theme and used a spring-inspired color palette of pink, yellow, and green to match the shop’s playful atmosphere.

Once the designs were approved, I hand-painted all four signs: Order Here, Take Out, Return Station, and Clean Up. I focused on keeping the visuals clear and readable from a distance, since the signs would be hanging above the counter. After installation, the owner shared that the signs helped employees direct customers more easily and improved how people moved through the space. The process emphasized collaboration, clarity, and making functional signage that still felt fun and welcoming.

The Outcome

The final signs successfully reduced confusion around tray returns and order pickup, making it easier for customers and delivery drivers to navigate the space. The playful, panda-themed designs fit naturally into the shop’s environment and were well received by both the owner and staff. Overall, the signage improved the flow of the store while maintaining a friendly and welcoming atmosphere.
