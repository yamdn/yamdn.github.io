Crystal Tide

Role Designer || Collaborative
Tools Rhino, Grasshopper, 3D printing
Watch this Youtube Short↗ on this project!

The Challenge

The main challenge of this project was finding the right shell shape while clearly highlighting the environmental statement. We wanted the piece to evoke the beauty of abalone shells and crystals while also referencing ocean pollution and plastic waste. Fabrication added another layer of complexity: we combined 3D printing with color filament to create the base shell and resin printing for the intricate, crystal-like details. At the same time, we aimed to raise awareness about environmental issues, using plastic filament waste in the process and presentation to reinforce the message. Balancing aesthetics, materials, and concept was the central challenge of the project.

The Approach

My partner and I started by making a mood board of things that represent both the beauty and ugliness of the environment. We were drawn to the way crystals and rocks grow, and that interest naturally expanded to seashells, especially abalone, which have unique colors and layered growth. When we looked closely at abalone shells, the surface reminded us of oil spills, which inspired us to explore the contrast between natural beauty and environmental damage. We then sketched several ideas and eventually settled on a curved shell form with a slimy, oil-like look. The shell was 3D printed using a greenish-blue shiny filament. Inside, the surface slopes slightly so the soap can be scraped and drained after use. The crystal element was resin printed so we could achieve more intricate details and exaggerate the sharp, almost painful quality of nature.

The Outcome

We presented the project at the end-of-year show, displaying all of our prototypes alongside the plastic waste generated during the process, which was later recycled. With over 200 people in attendance, visitors were able to touch and interact with the form and textures of the piece, helping them better understand both the material qualities and the environmental message behind the project. Many viewers were drawn to the contrast between the glossy shell and the sharp crystal, which often sparked conversations about pollution and ocean cleanup. Being able to physically handle the object encouraged people to slow down and engage more thoughtfully with the design. Overall, the project succeeded in turning a functional soap dish into a visual and physical reminder of environmental impact.
