Sesame's Hood

Role Lead Designer || Individual
Tools Rhino, CNC Woodwork, Laser Cutter
Visit the GitHub repo↗ for this project

The Challenge

One of the primary symptoms of Parkinson’s disease is tremors in a dominant arm. Tremors are defined as rhythmic oscillations in the frequency range of 3-5Hz. The treatment for these tremors is typically a dopamine boost, which transitions the patient from the “Off” state to the “On” state. However, if too much dopamine is provided, an additional symptom call dyskinesia occurs. This is characterized by rhythmic dance-like movements in the frequency range of 5-7Hz. It is essential to have this information to properly medicate a patient so that the patient is “On”, but not too “On”.

The Approach

I began the project by sketching three different designs on paper and refining them with measurements after getting feedback from my instructor and peers. To better visualize the concepts, I created three prototypes out of cardboard and hot glue, starting with a small, quick version and then moving to larger, life-size models. This allowed me to see where details could be added, how the structure would fit together, and how Sesame might interact with the space.

Once the prototypes were finalized, I moved into Rhino to create the digital design. The cat house included two window panels with cat and fish patterns, a roof panel with bubbly circular designs, a front panel featuring Sesame’s name, and a back panel with slots for organizing her toys. The structural panels were CNC cut with a dog-bone joint system for easy assembly, and the hammock was designed in a playful cat shape. After cutting, I assembled all the pieces, reinforced them with glue, and finished the house with wood oil to preserve it. The result was a functional, visually engaging cat house tailored to Sesame’s comfort and play needs.

The Outcome

Sesame immediately took to her new cat house, curling up in the cozy nook and lounging happily in the hammock. To make it even more comfortable, I added a heating pad so the space would feel warm and soft instead of hard. The toy organizer helped keep her favorite toys neatly in place, making playtime easier to manage. Overall, the cat house combined functionality, comfort, and playful design, creating a space that Sesame genuinely enjoys and that reflects her personality.
